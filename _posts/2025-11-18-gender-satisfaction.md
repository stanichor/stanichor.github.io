[Tailcalled](https://surveyanon.wordpress.com/) ran a "Gender satisfaction and other things" survey, so I decided to analyze the data. The responses came from 185 cis men recruited on Prolific. 

## Factor Structure

I was able to extract 9 factors: 
- Affective Gender Identity
- BDSM Interest
- Masculine Gender Norm Endorsement
- Masculine Gender Role Insecurity
- Gender Dysphoria
- Homosexuality
- Perceived Female Advantage
- Mental Illness
- Autogynephilia

This scale has high reliability for people with average and high interest in BDSM, it has low reliability for those with low interest in BDSM. Part of this is due to the number of respondents who, as far as the scale can tell, had essentially zero interest in BDSM. But, if we compare this scale to [the scale formed from Aella's Kink Survey](https://stanichor.net/kink-factor-analysis/#bdsm), we see that another issue is the limited number of items; increasing the number of items would likely improve reliability, even taking into account the low interest in BDSM.

### Partial Correlation Matrix

The partial correlation between two variables is their correlation after eliminating the influence of a third variable (or set of variables). [Inverting a covariance matrix](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Covariance_matrix#Inverse_of_the_covariance_matrix) yields its precision matrix, from which partial correlations and partial variances can be extracted. The resulting partial correlations are those between two variables while controlling for the influence of *every other* variable. The resulting partial variances reflect how much variance remains unexplained by all the other variables.

<div style="text-align: center;">
    <figure>
        <img src="/assets/images/gender-satisfaction/partial-factor-corr.png" width="1000" alt="Alt text">
    </figure>
</div>

Compared to the correlation matrix, the partial correlation matrix has much smaller correlations. This suggests that the Markov network describing the interaction between the factors is sparser than it appears when examining the raw correlation matrix.

The BDSM Interest factor has notable partial correlations with: 
- Homosexuality (+.23)
- Autogynephilia (+.22)
- Masculine Gender Norm Endorsement (+.19)
- Masculine Gender Role Insecurity (+.18)

Given that the BDSM Interest, Homosexuality, and Autogynephilia factors all consist of questions about sexual arousal, there may be a general sexual arousal (horniness) factor that links them all together. That would also explain the partial correlation between BDSM Interest and Masculine Gender Norm Endorsement. The association between BDSM Interest and Masculine Gender Role Insecurity remains unclear; it may be noise.
